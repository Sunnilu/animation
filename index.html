Claymation, a type of stop-motion animation, originated with the invention of plasticine clay in the late 19th century, which allowed for easily molded and slow-drying clay figures that could be manipulated for animation; the term "claymation" itself was coined by animator Will Vinton in the 1970s.

The Little Drummer Boy is a stop motion television special produced by Rankin/Bass Productions, based on the song of the same name. It was first televised in Canada on December 19, 1968, on the CTV Television Network, followed four days later by its American nationwide telecast on NBC. A sequel was broadcast in 1976.

Aaron, a Jewish boy living peacefully on a farm, loses his parents to bandits. Traumatized, he vows to hate humanity. Forced into a traveling circus, he initially rebels. However, witnessing the Magi's journey and their kindness, he begins to soften. After escaping with his animals, he joins the Magi in Bethlehem. Unable to offer a traditional gift, Aaron plays his drum for the newborn baby, a gesture that heals his lamb and restores his faith in humanity.
